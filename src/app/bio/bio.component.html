About Me

My name is Zuyin (Alvin) Zheng (郑祖荫 in Chinese). I’m a job candidate expected in May 2019 at Department of Management Information Systems, Fox Business School, Temple University.

My research focuses on how to retain customers, facilitate engagement and promote value-creating interactions in online environments, such as online labor markets, crowdfunding, and mobile apps from the perspective of platform design. In my research, I use quantitative methods including econometrics, field experiments, and natural experiments to answer real-life business questions by closely cooperating with industry practitioners. I strive to conduct research that is theoretically appealing to scholars while practically relevant to practitioners and managers. My current research falls into two main streams: two-sided platforms and app economy.

I have teaching experience in undergraduate as independent instructor and graduate (PhD and Executive DBA) as either instructor or teaching assistant. Specifically, I taught undergraduate Data Analytics, which includes three topics: database design and SQL, data visualization, and advanced analytics in R. I also taught a workshop Python and Web Data Extraction, which was open to both PhD students and faculty. I have also been working as a teaching assistant for several doctoral seminars, quantitative methods courses and Executive DBA seminars.

I have been working as the chief data scientist for an app company with 50 million global users since June 2017. I also worked with a consulting team for a Fortunate 500 software company in Silicon Valley in 2015 and 2016.
